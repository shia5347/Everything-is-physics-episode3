    Basics of work

    For the basics of work, I have learned that work is measured in unit of 'Joules' - that has been originated from honouring James Prescott Joule. For calculating the work based off throwing an object, I know to use the equation "W = FcosΘ(deltaDistance)" where F is the magnitude of the force, (deltaDistance) is the distance from the throwing object's starting position and "lifting off" position", and finally cosΘ the angle between the throw direction and distance. However for pushing an object against a slope, the cosΘ would be changed to a sinΘ.

    Mechanical work (still the basics)

    I have learned that something can be considered a "Mechanical work" if there is both Displacement and Force properties on an moving object.

    Gravitational Potential Energy basics

    The Gravitational Potential energy is the Potential energy stored in an object that is being held up (being held away from the ground), where its potential energy depends on factors like height and mass of object.

    Example

    Determining the work for falling or moving object

    For a moving object you would simply, use the equation W = Fg(delta height), where (delta height) represents the speed of object and Fg the natural gravity force. For the falling object you would start the equation with (delta Eg) since work = (delta Eg). The equation is (delta Eg) = mg(deltaHeight).
